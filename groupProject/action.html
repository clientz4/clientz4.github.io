Top 10 Ways to Help Sustain Local Lakes


#5: Consider Your Cleaning Waste

Conserve water and use phosphate-free soap to maintain the health of local lakes.


#6: Consult With Construction Workers

When building a property, try not to destroy native plants that help maintain and purify local lakes.


#7: Develop Awareness About Your Lawn Care Services

Make sure the chemicals used by your lawn care company are minimally toxic.

#1: Think before dumping

Every piece of litter makes it into the lake, in one way or another.


#2: Seek Alternatives For Pesticides

Pesticides contaminate lake water, so instead seek out other ways to shield yourself against pests.


#3: Use Less Fertilizer

Fertilizer is often overused, which ends up polluting lakes.


#4: Nurture Native Plant Species

Native plants provide habitats and food for wildlife, aren’t high maintenance and are better at reducing local pollution than invasive plants.




blank space


#8: Release Endangered Species

Catching and keeping endangered species will hinder their reproductive progress.


#9: Exterminate Invasive Plants

Get rid of any invasive flora such as loosestrife that are harming the lakes’ ecosystems.


#10: Provide Help Locally

There are many organizations that would appreciate YOUR volunteer help. Save Lake Sammamish (425-641-3008) hosts lake cleanup days periodically. The city of Bellevue’s Stream Team (425-452-5200) and King County's Surface Water Management Division (206-477-4800) continue to plant and maintain the health of native vegetation on lakes and riversides, along with preserving the actual bodies of water.
